How is Kipchoge Score calculated?

Eliud Kipchoge set the marathon world record of 2:00:35 at the Berlin Marathon on September 25, 2022 — the first man ever to run a sub-2:01 marathon officially. His average pace: 2:51 /km (5.83 m/s).

Your Kipchoge Score = your fastest average pace (from runs ≥ 1 km) ÷ Kipchoge's WR pace × 100. It shows what percentage of his speed you're currently hitting.

For context:

🚶 Walk~24%
🏃 Recreational35–52%
🏃 Intermediate52–68%
🏃 Advanced68–80%
🏅 Sub-2:30 marathon80–95%
🐐 Kipchoge100%

We only use runs ≥ 1 km to exclude warm-up fragments and GPS glitches. The score reflects your best recent effort, not a race PR.
